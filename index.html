About Me

I bridge the gap between biological movement and high-performance animation through quantitative pipeline engineering. For the past seven years, my work has focused on the biomechanical reconstruction of animal locomotion, building end-to-end pipelines that translate complex biological data such as X-ray motion capture and 3D mesh scans into functional kinematic animations and predictive models.

My approach combines a deep understanding of vertebrate anatomy with rigorous computational tools. More recently, I have applied my background in 3D geometry and biomechanics to the evolving AI landscape. I have contributed as a technical consultant for industry-leading AI companies (via Handshake), where I design and review high-fidelity training data and PhD-level reasoning benchmarks to stress-test model capabilities in biology, physics, and graphic arts.

Technical Stack: Maya, Blender, Unity, Python (Trimesh, NumPy, PyTorch), R (nlme, RRPP, ggplot).
