About Me

I am a newly minted PhD from the Pierce Lab at Harvard University. My doctoral dissertation investigated the evolutionary origins of the mammalian hip joint through an interdiscplinary lens, integrating methods from paleobiology, experimental biomechanics, and developmental biology. I am question-driven, adaptable, and a quick learner. Across my research projects, the common throughline is a knack for designing appropriate experiments to collect the best data suited to the research question and strategic decision-making on how to gain statistical insights from the data collected. I am experienced in R with occasional use of Python and MATLAB.
