I am a PhD Candidate in the Pierce Lab at Harvard University. I am interested in the evolution of locomotor systems, and my doctoral work focuses on the sprawling-to-parasagittal transition in synapsids with an emphasis on the hindlimb. To investigate this topic, my research integrates functional morphology, phylogenetic comparative methods, and experimental biomechanics. I also have an interest in pedagogy and have teaching experience ranging from human to comparative anatomy, cadaveric to lab-based, including syllabus design as a primary instructor.

I am currently searching for majority-teaching roles that include the possibility of pursuing independent research.
