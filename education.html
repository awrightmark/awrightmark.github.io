My educational trajectory began in college with an interest in anatomy. While working as a teaching assistant for a cadaveric lab, I realized I was a lot more interested in how structures came to be as compared to the medical interests of many of my colleagues. During my master's program, I developed a strong interest in the evolutionary component. During my PhD, I am finally synthesizing these interests - taking an aspect of anatomy very relevant to humans today (our posture and limbs) and exploring the evolutionary journey in deep time. My full educational trajectory is at the bottom of this page.
