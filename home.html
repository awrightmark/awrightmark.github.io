Mark A. Wright

Functional Morphologist ⚬ Paleontologist ⚬ Data Scientist

PhD Student, Stephanie E. Pierce Lab at Harvard University

Research Interests

  • The evolutionary origins of mammals and how you get from early amniotes to the mammalian body plan
  • How do similar ecomorphologies arise independently? Is evolution predictable?
  • To what extent can the locomotor capabilities of fossil animals be reasonably reconstructed? How can we animate animal locomotion so that it feels "real"?

Other Interests

  • Traveling, culture, identity, and language learning
  • Film and narrative in visual media
  • Labor organizing in academia and how we can make science more open, accessible, and safe

Classes I would like to teach one day

  • From Fossil to Phylogeny: The Fundamentals of Understanding Evolution
  • Vertebrate Forms: Major Anatomical Transformations
  • Animal Locomotion: The Physics of Movement
  • Primordial Soup to Primitive Society: A Linear Story of How We Got to Humans
  • Fossil Animation: Reconstructing Dynamic Movements in Long Dead Animals
