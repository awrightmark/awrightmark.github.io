Research Interests

  • How do similar ecomorphologies arise independently? Is evolution predictable?
  • To what extent can the locomotor capabilities of fossil animals be reasonably reconstructed? How can we animate animal locomotion so that it feels "real"?

Hello, Internet Traveler! My name is Mark A. Wright, and I am a doctoral student in the Stephanie E. Pierce Lab at Harvard University. I am broadly interested in the evolution of morphology and function, particularly as it relates to the origin and diversification of mammals. My work focuses primarily on the hindlimb, and I incorporate various techniques, including musculoskeletal dissections, biomechanical modeling, and geometric morphometrics into my research.
