Research Interests

My research is interdisciplinary, integrating functional morphology, phylogenetic comparative methods, experimental biomechanics, and select methods from developmental biology to understand how locomotor systems evolve across millions of years of geologic time. I am primarily a morphologist (more on the phenotype than the genotype side of the continuum) and really enjoy developing R code to analyze complex, multivariate datasets. I work with both extant and extinct species and have spent a lot of time in museum collections around the world. My previous work has included sensory systems (olfaction in myrmecophagous mammals) as well.

Moving forward, I hope to continue studying the evolution of locomotor systems with more recently diverged groups, such as individual clades of primates or carnivorans, and continue moving towards whole-body frameworks for understanding locomotion rather than examining individual body regions or structures in isolation.
